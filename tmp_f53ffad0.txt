H-infinity based Extended Kalman Filter for State Estimation in Highly
Non-linear Soft Robotic System
Junn Yong Loo1 , Chee Pin Tan1 and Surya Girinatha Nurzaman1
Abstract‚Äî Sensor data play a significant role in the control of
robotic systems. While soft robotics is promising for operation
in unstructured environments, it is difficult to integrate sensors
into soft robots because their inherent softness can be disturbed
by the use of sensors. One way to overcome this challenge
is to use an observer/filter to estimate the variables (states)
that would have been measured by those sensors. Nevertheless,
applying an observer/filter scheme to a soft robot introduces
challenges due to the high non-linearity in its system model.
In this paper, a novel H‚àû based Extended Kalman Filter
(EKF) is proposed to estimate the states of a soft continuum
manipulator and its performances are investigated. The H‚àû EKF are tested with model simulations of an experimentally
validated soft continuum manipulator system with highly nonlinear kinematics and dynamics. The results show that H‚àû -EKF
achieves accurate estimations in pneumatic muscle actuator
(pMA)‚Äôs elongation and manipulator‚Äôs task space coordinates
while estimation result for elongation rate is less satisfactory
due to large model uncertainties.

I. INTRODUCTION
Soft robots are promising for adaptive operation in unstructured environments because of their inherent compliance
that is typically found in biological organisms [1]. A subclass
of soft robots, soft continuum manipulators have gained
much attention in the past decade due to their soft properties and hyper-redundancy, allowing dexterous motion not
feasible with the conventional rigid robots. Generally, there
are two types of soft continuum manipulators, tendon-driven
and pneumatic [2]. Examples of tendon-driven manipulators
are for instance the elephant trunk manipulator designed by
Hannan and Walker that grabs object by bending its structure
[3]. Renda et. al. designed an octopus-like continuum arm
prototype that is driven by 12 Cosserat rod embedded inside
its silicon body [4]. A snake-like, wire-actuated medical
robot device with multiple NiTi backbones was designed
in [5] for minimally invasive surgery. As for pneumatic
manipulators, Bartow et. al. designed a novel continuum
trunk actuated by braided pneumatic artificial muscles in [6].
A multi-section continuum robots with continuous backbone
called OctArm was created in [7]. Marchese and Rus detailed
a fabrication process for its soft fluidic manipulator that
composed entirely of low durometer elastomer in [8].
In robotics, sensory data play a significant role in system
controllability and robustness [2]. Several examples of task

space tracking require intermediate states such as continuum
section length, joint space velocity and section curvature,
in configuration space as control feedback states. However,
inherent softness in soft robots challenges the conventional
ways of integrating sensors in them for spatial configuration detection. There are several types of sensor used in
soft robotic systems. For examples, cable potentiometers
[9] and string encoders [10] for actuator/joint space vector
(actuator length), encoders and magnetic sensors [11] for
configuration states (segment curvature and bending angle),
vision [8], [12]‚Äì[17] and electromagnetic sensors [18] for
task space position. Other examples include in-line load
cell [19], [20] for force estimation and pressure sensor
[9] for pressure regulation. However, equipping soft robots
with sensors could have negative effects. For example, a
cable potentiometer induces tension and friction that could
stiffen the soft robot, constricting its reachability and causes
deviation to the established models. Sensors like encoders
and load cells that are rigid introduce extra weights which
need to be considered. These sensors could be subjected to
noise as well. Vision captured data on the other hand, do not
usually contain noise but involve computationally expensive
algorithm when calculating complex robot geometry with
visual markers. With the intention of reducing the number of
sensors involved, an alternative is to substitute sensors with
a dynamical observer/filter scheme for state estimation.
To utilize an observer/filter or state estimator, a system
model is required. However, the kinematics and dynamics
of soft robotic systems are very different from the conventional rigid robots. Unlike rigid articulated manipulators, continuum manipulators have flexible structures and
hyper-redundant actuated DOFs which present a formidable
challenge for modelling their true non-linearity [2]. The
success of practical soft robot control heavily hinges on
the development of these kinematic and dynamic models.
While model-free approaches exist, it is difficult to perform
stability analysis and convergence proofs on them due to
black box nature of the model-free approaches, making their
efficacy difficult to analyze [2]. For well-structured and
compact manipulators in known environment, model-based
methods generally still provide more accurate results [2].
For the intention of developing a model-based observer/filter,
existing kinematic and dynamic modelling techniques of soft
continuum manipulators are reviewed.
The majority of the kinematics are derived from the
elementary Constant Curvature (CC) model or Variable Constant Curvature (VCC) approximation, while most of the
dynamics used either the Euler-Lagrangian or Lagrangian

in its nascent stage. Due to the fact that kinematic models are
difficult to develop, a dynamic formulation based on the less

uncertainties [2]. Renda et. al. [22] developed a dynamic
model for the octopus inspired continuum soft robot arm
described in [4]. The model was validated with experiment
data from a working prototype and proved to be usable
as a dynamic simulation platform for control strategy of
continuum robot arm. Godage et al. [16] presented a novel
spatial kinematic model based on mode shape functions,
for a multi-section continuum arms robot that resembles
the OctArm. The method employed forward and inverse
kinematics to model spatial bending and elongation of the
continuum robot. A novel spatial dynamics of the complex
curve parametric arm model was introduced in [17] based
on the structurally accurate modal kinematics derived, where
a computational scheme designed to compute the equations
of motion (EoM) was also presented. Marchese and Rus
developed a multi-segment kinematic model [8] by incorporating the stresses and strains in the elastomer into the piecewise curvature approximation, which was then extended to
a dynamic model [15] with its control algorithm presented
for trajectory optimization. Falkenhaln et. al. developed a
practicable lumped mass dynamic model associated with its
kinematic model for fast simulation of the Bionic Handling
Assistant [23], which became the basis for a model-based
controller later in [9].
To reduce the need of integrating various type and number
of sensors, an alternative is to use an observer/filter scheme
for state estimation. In [24], the performance of adaptive
EKF on a tendon-driven soft continuum manipulator system
was compared to the experiment data of the optical fiber
curvature sensor and accelerometers, however, the EKF was
based upon a simplified quasi-static model. In [25], the
conventional Luenberger observer was used to estimate the
position and velocity vectors in the reduced order steadystate model. The large scale model before order reduction
was obtained through finite element method (FEM) and linearized at the desired soft robot configuration as equilibrium
point. Nevertheless, there are not many studies on state
estimation for soft robots, and hence this area is still very
much in its infancy with much potential for development.
In this paper, the pneumatic soft continuum manipulator in
[16], [17] (with highly non-linearity whose its models have
been experimentally verified) is used. With the soft manipulator‚Äôs system model available, we proposed a discrete H‚àû
based EKF (hereafter known as H‚àû -EKF) to estimate the
states of the manipulator, and analyze on its capabilities to
cope with the soft robot‚Äôs high degree of non-linearity. The
proposed discrete-time H‚àû -EKF minimizes the worst-case
estimation error and offers a higher robustness against the
system noises [26], where its continuous-time version was
shown in [27].

Despite the fact that H‚àû -EKF can estimate elongations
and task space coordinates accurately, the estimation results
of elongation rates with significant errors are barely satisfactory. This is due to the large model discretization error
at a large sampling time step of 0.001s, coupled with the
reduced MHTM order in kinematic model. Furthermore, this
is aggravated by the fact that dynamic model is derived from
the kinematic model. Simulating the system model at a time
step of 0.001s drives the states to infinity since high nonlinearity in this soft manipulator model requires the sampling
time step to be small for the system to be stable, especially
when elongation rate spikes during system input alternation.
Due to large system model uncertainties, Œ∑ is forced to
kept small to satisfy condition (12) because of the state
estimate covariance, Pk that propagates and converges at a
large value. This restricts the optimality of H‚àû -EKF since
the estimation errors are bounded at a larger performance
bound in (11).
V. CONCLUSIONS
With the state space model formulated in Section II, the
discrete state estimation filter, H‚àû -EKF are developed and
implemented on the non-linear soft continuum manipulator
system. Due to high non-linearity of the soft robot system,
reduction in MHTM order and sampling frequency leads
to large model uncertainties. Hence, H‚àû -EKF is proposed
with the assumption that it is more robust against the model
uncertainties compared to the conventional EKF.
The states (elongations and elongation rates of the sectional pMAs) are estimated and validated via comparing
them with the system model simulation as ground truths. The
results of pMA elongation estimation are satisfactory where
the errors are minimal in H‚àû -EKF. From there, accurate
manipulator‚Äôs task space configurations can be detected,
leading to proprioception if manipulator tip‚Äôs coordinates
are available (as the reference measurement input for filter).
Using the proposed state estimation observer/filter scheme,
the amount of sensors required to measure elongations, elongation rates and task space coordinates can then be reduced

However, the elongation rate estimation results are less

fold decrease in sampling frequency, also by the reduced
MHTM order in kinematic model. Hence, a more robust
non-linear estimation filter/observer such as Robust H‚àû
Kalman filter [30] or Sliding Mode observer [31], [32] can
be implemented in the future to deal explicitly with the
high model uncertainties, coupled with the high system nonlinearity. Since the work in this paper are carried out solely
on simulation platform, and the fact that filter‚Äôs reference
measurement i.e. the task space coordinates from vision capture system might be time-delayed in real-life, the presented
estimation scheme is planned to be experimentally validated
with a working soft manipulator prototype.
